# GEO + SEO Checklist (Generative Engine Optimization + Search)

> Clarification for all departments: In our content workflow, **GEO = Generative Engine Optimization** (LLM/AI-answer optimization), **not** geofencing or country restriction.
> Use “international/localization” language only when region routing or hreflang is intentionally part of the article scope.

## 1) GEO (LLM optimization) basics
- Use answer-first intros (2–4 sentence direct answer near top).
- Add concise comparison bullets and decision criteria for easy AI extraction.
- Include clear entities (tool names, versions, workflows, constraints).
- Keep claims verifiable and avoid absolute wording.
- Add structured FAQ sections for high-intent questions.

## 2) URL structure (only for internationalized pages)
- Use country/language routes consistently (`/us/`, `/uk/`, `/fr/`, `/ma-fr/`, `/ar/`, `/ma-ar/`).
- Keep one canonical per page variant.

## 3) hreflang
- Add hreflang tags in page `<head>`.
- Mirror hreflang alternates in sitemap.
- Always include `x-default`.

## 4) Localization
- Translate title, meta description, H1, CTA, and FAQ per locale.
- Adapt examples, currency, and wording to region.

## 5) Technical
- Submit sitemap to Google Search Console.
- Set geo targets where appropriate (if using ccTLD/subdomains).
- Ensure fast performance globally (CDN + compressed assets).

## 6) Conversion
- Show locale switcher.
- Default locale from browser language, but allow manual override.
- Keep forms and legal text localized.

## 7) Analytics
- Track conversions by locale and country.
- Compare opt-in and paid waitlist rates per market.
